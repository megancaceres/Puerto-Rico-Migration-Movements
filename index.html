Relocations From Puerto Rico to the U.S. Mainland Post Hurricane Maria and Irma.

By: Megan Caceres

Dec 13, 2020

Out of all the islands in the Caribbean, Puerto Rico endured the overwhelming brunt of two major hurricanes within the same year, months apart.

The impact of the storms was not only felt on the island, but was also felt on the United States Mainland, where thousands of Puerto Ricans relocated and settled post hurricane Maria and Irma.

With increased mobility of people across the United States Mainland, however, a weather event that takes place in the Caribbean affects communities not just there, but also far and away.

Using data collected by the United States Census Bureau, with data stemming from the American Community Survey, we concluded that this massive and continuing post-Maria and Irma exodus of Puerto Ricans, has led to an overall increase in the number of Puerto Ricans in the United States overall. Given the importance that population size and growth have in the social and political discourse in the United States, this increase will contribute to make Puerto Ricans more visible politically, socially, culturally and economically.

The primary relocation destination for most Puerto Ricans has been Florida, but we also saw relocation patterns in Pennsylvania, New York, and Massachusetts.
